Manufacturing Management

── Product Engineering & PLM [2R · 2P · 1A] 5 caps
  1. 1) Bill of Materials (BOM) generic ✓ — Does your solution maintain multi-level BOM hierarchies with component relationships, alternate and substitute variants, phantom sub-assemblies, and automatic cost roll-up to finished goods, so you can plan production accurately and manage engineering changes?
  2. 2) Routings, Operations & Work Centers generic ✓ — Does your solution manage ordered manufacturing operation sequences with work centre capacity and cost data, assign resources with skill requirements, record setup and run times, support alternate paths, and maintain routing versions with effectivity dates, so you can plan production accurately and adapt to process changes?
  3. 3) Engineering Change Management (ECO / ECR) 2/3 sectors — Does your solution manage engineering change requests through formal approval workflows, assess impact on product structure and routings, maintain audit trails, and support multiple effectivity methods, so you can implement product changes safely and compliantly?
  4. 4) Product Master & Document Management (Basic) 2/3 sectors — Does your solution maintain an item master with revision control, attach engineering documents, track item status through lifecycle stages, and manage manufacturing process information so you have a complete product record?
  5. 5) Enterprise PLM (Advanced) 2/3 sectors — Does your solution compare item revisions, classify and search items by attributes, manage technical documentation, track compliance records, and integrate with external PLM systems?
── Production Execution (MES) [2R · 5P] 7 caps
  1. 1) Work Orders & Job Cards generic ✓ — Does your solution create and manage work orders against bills of materials and routings, automatically reserve components, track status through execution, and record labour time and scrap per operation, so production can be scheduled and monitored end-to-end?
  2. 2) Production Scheduling & Capacity Planning generic ✓ — Does your solution schedule work orders to work centres respecting finite capacity and operation sequence, with visual load display, automatic bottleneck identification, and conflict detection, so planners can balance demand against available capacity?
  3. 3) Shop Floor Execution & MES generic ✓ — Does your solution capture operator actions at the work centre, including start/stop events with timestamps, material scans, quality measurements, and downtime reasons, with real-time alerts for disruptions and specification breaches, so you have complete shop-floor visibility and traceability?
  4. 4) Process & Batch Manufacturing 2/3 sectors — Does your solution manage production using recipes and formulas with batch-level tracking, yield measurement, by-product and co-product capture, genealogy traceability, and potency and shelf-life attributes, so you can manufacture and trace variable-yield batches with full compliance visibility?
  5. 5) Mixed-Mode & Configure-to-Order Manufacturing 2/3 sectors — Does your solution manage multiple manufacturing modes—discrete, repetitive, make-to-stock, make-to-order, engineer-to-order, and configure-to-order—with automatic bill of materials generation and mode-specific cost calculation, so you can fulfil diverse customer orders without switching systems?
  6. 6) IoT, Machine Integration & Data Collection 2/3 sectors — Can your solution connect to production equipment using standard industrial protocols, capture machine status and cycle data automatically, store time-series telemetry for performance analysis, and generate real-time alerts on equipment anomalies, so you eliminate manual data entry and gain visibility into production performance?
  7. 7) Labour Tracking & Shop Floor Time Management generic ✓ — Does your solution capture operator time against work orders, distinguish labour categories, export to payroll, and compare actual hours against standard time per operation, so labour efficiency and job costs are tracked accurately?
── Planning & Costing (Manufacturing) [3R · 3P] 6 caps
  1. 1) Material Requirements Planning (MRP) generic ✓ — Does your solution consolidate demand from sales orders and forecasts, explode multi-level BOMs against inventory and on-order quantities, apply lot-sizing and safety-stock policies, and generate planned orders with lead-time offsetting and exception alerts, so you can meet customer demand without stock-outs or excess inventory?
  2. 2) Inventory & Work-in-Progress Tracking generic ✓ — Does your solution track raw materials, work-in-progress, and finished goods separately, maintain lot and serial traceability through production, manage bin locations across facilities, automatically consume materials on work order completion, and support quality holds and quarantine areas, so you have full visibility and control of inventory throughout your manufacturing process?
  3. 3) Product Costing & Variance Analysis generic ✓ — Does your solution calculate standard and actual product costs from material, labour, and overhead, decompose variances by type, and revalue inventory when costs change, so planners and accountants have accurate cost visibility and variance reporting?
  4. 4) Subcontract & Outsourced Manufacturing 2/3 sectors — Send materials to an external manufacturer who performs part or all of the production and returns finished or semi-finished goods. Distinct from a simple purchase because the organisation owns the material throughout.
  5. 5) Demand Planning & Forecasting generic ✓ — Does your solution generate statistical forecasts using multiple methods, aggregate demand across dimensions, track forecast consumption against actual sales, and measure accuracy with standard metrics, so you can align supply planning with realistic demand signals?
  6. 6) Rough-Cut Capacity & S&OP Integration 2/3 sectors — Does your solution model rough-cut capacity constraints against critical resources, maintain resource load profiles, and generate the master production schedule from your S&OP plan, so bottlenecks are visible before detailed MRP planning begins?
── Quality & Compliance (QMS) [3R · 1P] 4 caps
  1. 1) Quality Management (QM) generic ✓ — Does your solution manage quality inspections across incoming, in-process, and final stages, record non-conformances with corrective-action workflows, maintain audit trails linked to lot or serial number, and generate certificates of conformance, so you can demonstrate compliance and prevent defective goods reaching customers?
  2. 2) Lot/Serial Traceability & Genealogy 3/3 sectors — Does your solution track material lots bidirectionally through production and supply chain, maintain serial numbers per unit, generate recall lists by lot and customer, display genealogy across assembly levels, and monitor expiry dates, so you execute rapid recalls and prove product safety to regulators?
  3. 3) Manufacturing Safety, EHS & Regulatory Compliance generic ✓ — Does your solution manage hazardous materials on bills of materials, attach safety documentation to components, define PPE and lockout/tagout requirements per operation, track worker certifications, and capture safety incidents with corrective actions, so compliance risks and worker safety gaps are identified before harm occurs?
  4. 4) Energy, Utility & Sustainability Tracking 3/3 sectors — Does your solution track utility consumption by production line, calculate emissions per unit, allocate energy costs to work orders, and monitor resource streams, so sustainability reporting obligations are met with auditable data?
── Manufacturing Maintenance & Analytics [3P · 3A] 6 caps
  1. 1) OEE & Manufacturing Performance Analytics generic ✓ — Does your solution calculate Overall Equipment Effectiveness from availability, performance, and quality metrics, display dashboards accessible to shopfloor and mobile users, drill down across organisational hierarchy, analyse downtime with trend identification, and alert when KPI thresholds are breached, so plant managers identify bottlenecks and optimise production?
  2. 2) Repair, Rework & Returned-Goods Manufacturing 2/3 sectors — Does your solution manage repair orders with warranty classification, disassembly specifications, RMA linkage, and separate cost tracking, so returned goods are processed without restarting production?
  3. 3) Maintenance Integration (CMMS / EAM Linkage) 3/3 sectors — Does your solution link production work centres to asset maintenance records, automatically reserve capacity for planned maintenance, create work requests from equipment faults, and display unified production and maintenance schedules, so production planners optimise scheduling around equipment reliability?
  4. 4) Digital Thread & Manufacturing Digital Twin 2/3 sectors — Can your solution maintain a continuous digital record linking design, manufacturing specifications, and as-built data per unit, simulate production schedules to forecast cycle times, and provide operator training simulation, so end-to-end traceability and workflow optimisation are achieved without disrupting live production?
  5. 5) Multi-Site & Inter-Company Manufacturing generic ✓ — Can your solution coordinate production across multiple sites, standardise bills of material and routings, optimise material flow between plants, and calculate available-to-promise across your network, so you fulfil orders from your entire manufacturing footprint?
  6. 6) AI / ML for Manufacturing 2/3 sectors — Does your solution detect equipment anomalies and forecast defect rates from production data, analyse historical runs for yield optimisation, and generate AI-driven scheduling recommendations, so manufacturers can intervene early and optimise output?
